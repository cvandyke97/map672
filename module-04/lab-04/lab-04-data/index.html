Well, not every location on this map is a repository for mine tailings, toxic sediments, and other pollutants. Really, that's restricted to the Upper Clark Fork Watershed, which runs from Butte northwest to Missoula. It is the largest Superfund complex in the United States. The valley is even visible on this map! My dissertation research looked at the remediation and restoration of the Clark Fork River near Milltown, which is about 10 km upstream of Missoula – the restoration project, which was 30 years in the making, involved removal of Milltown Dam, the excavation of toxic sediments that had accumulated behind the dam throughout the 20th century, and reconstruction of the river channel and floodplain.
